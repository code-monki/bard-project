Eyeball Beta- Class Probe

design by Nick Bradbeer

The Eyeball series of probes offered a means of observing over a wide area without danger to the parent ship. Ranging in size from 1dt to 3 dt, plus the larger 5dt Cyclops, they provided survey and scout ships with many additional functions. Later versions of the probes were modified to provide even more capabilities, including personnel transport and some were even made jump capable.
The Beta version was a slight improvement over the Alpha version, intended for close in Search and Rescue work. G-Hours of fuel were sacrificed to include a wider range of communicators, including short range laser and radio links, as well as a 25m Neural Activity Sensor to scan for life signs. Main comms were, however, still run through the 1000AU military maser link.
EMM was fitted as standard, since this was primarily a military design, and the probes were even fitted out with Contra-Grav drives to assist operations in planetary gravity wells.

See also the Alpha Series Search Probe and the Gamma Series Rescue Probe.
