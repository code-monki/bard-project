Gargoyle Battle Rider

design by Pete Grey

The Gargoyle BR was originally designed to be the companion piece for the Third Imperiums controversial Gishikimega class Light Cruiser. Unlike most battle rider and tender combinations, the 30,000 dton Gishikimega was intended to be a main battle unit, with its five Gargoyle riders escorting and screening it into the heat of battle. The idea of using the tender as more than a largely helpless support unit ran against the strong tide of conventional thinking that always stifled the Imperial Navy, and only half of the intended class ever reached production due to bureaucratic stonewalling.

The combination recieved its baptism of fire during the Solomani Rim War, and acquited itself rather well against light Solomani fleet elements. Nevertheless the design was a political orphan, and went out of production in 1023. Most of the class was banished to outlying frontier areas, and a good number of them ended up in the Domain of Deneb. Several served in the Fourth and Fifth Frontier Wars with distinction, where the shortage of new line units forced the Domain naval command to deploy them in spite of their prejudice. Both ships were still in service by the time of the Civil War and the Collapse, and served with distinction against Virus.

The strategic value of small battle riders in the defensive role became clear after the Rape of Trin. The Regency Navy decided that employing large numbers of 1k to 10k ton riders in place of traditional SDBs in critical systems bordering the Wilds would would be more effective against large Vampire incursions. The Gargoyle was one of several BR classes rushed back into production, but the cheapness of producing the class meant that its construction would outstrip the supply of available Gishikimegas. With no other recourse the Navy modified a number of older frigate, escort and destroyer vessels to act as carriers, and who would supplement the BRs firepower with their own.

The vessel has an unusually large cargo hold, the result of a design specification for local customisation. The vessel is designed with a minimum level of embedded firepower, with local commanders filling out the balance of the vessel as they saw fit. This was not only intended to provide ships to order for local conditions, but also to inject a level of uncertainty by randomising each ship's combat abilities and making their actual equippage unpredicatble.
