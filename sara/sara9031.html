Sabre Expediter Courier

design by Peter Gray

The Sabre Expediter is a private design first built for a number of mid-sized courier companies operating in the Spinward Marches. Given the slow and arbitrary nature of the defunct Imperial X-Boat system, a booming market in quick data transference existed in many frontier areas. The Expediter served mostly in the rimward parts of the Marches, linking worlds like Trin and Glisten with the scattered Imperial outposts and industrial worlds of Trojan Reach. When the Aslan invaded during the Civil War period, the companies that owned or leased these vessels were driven deeper into the Domain of Deneb, and the ship became a common sight along many main trade arteries along the Midway and the Stronghold. The lower maintenance costs and greater flexibility of the design have subsequently made it a mainstay of the new X-Web, with several hundred working primarily in the interior of Deneb and along the Reft Highway.

The vessels main competitive edge over the old X-Boats was its J-5 ability, enabling to transfer data at a faster rate over long distances. Limited passenger and for high priority mail and freight made these ships even more valuable. The vessel was originally designed to be unarmed, but all examples of the class constructed in the last twenty years now carry two hardpoints. The gunner listed in the crew complement is usually not carried on unarmed versions, and the extra stateroom is freed up for additional passengers or cargo. Like most couriers, the vessel carries an additional computer for data handling and storage. The vessel has been a godsend for the Regency and its overstretched communications; the one complaint has centered around its limited reaction mass carriage and low Heplar endurance. Some crews have also complained about its inability to carry high impact weaponry.
