Aganar Light Cruiser

design by Idiot/Savant

"The first Aganar class light cruiser was built by the Imperial Glisten Navy in 997. The last of the sixty vessels was commissioned in 1029. The ships are named for famous battles of history. At the outbreak of the Fifth Frontier War, the Aganars were the oldest class of vessels still in service with the IGN, and were committed to action immediately as one of the few meson gun carrier classes in use. Merely the technological equals of their opponents in the Collace Independence Fleet, and heavily outnumbered at every encounter due to the vagaries of their pre-war deployment, the Aganar squadrons were virtually annihilated in the opening weeks of the war. Between combat losses and the destruction of a number of damaged ships under repair during the brief Collace occupation of Glisten, only a single ship out of the sixty, the IGNS Alesia, survived through the end of the war. The Alesia was reclassified as a fleet escort during the fleet reorganization of 1112, and was still in service as a training vessel as of 1118."
    -- The Dean Files

This is a FF&S reworking of a design by R.S. Dean (MegaTraveller Design). The MT version had a ludicrously short endurance of < 2 weeks (or 3 days in combat); this version isn't nearly as tight, but still manages to mount a useful sized meson gun.

The best description of the Aganar-class is "an eggshell armed with a hammer". While its main armament can swat smaller escorts and pose a threat to some larger vessels, the Aganar has thin armour and is vulnerable to attacks by PAWs. Because of this, it is probably best utilised as a raider rather than in stand-up fleet actions.

Except for a handful of officers, the entire crew is accomodated at double-occupancy. A minimal hanger is installed, and a 40-DspT Pinnace serves as a general utility vehicle.
