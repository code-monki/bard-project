LSP MP-12B Mining Platform

design by Peter Gray

The LSP MP-12B is typical of antebellum era Mining Platforms. Designed for the unglamorous job of finding, mining and processing asteroidal ore bodies for eventual refining and working, these vessels are generally ugly in appearance and downright crude in construction. The MP-12B was LSP's standard TL-12 mining platform, designed for maximum maintainability and ease of obtaining replacement parts. Crew comfort is at a minimum, with most sleeping on bunks, and often hot bunking with the crews of docked Seekers. The ship carries nineteen Prospecting Bubbles to do ore body surveys, plant explosives and basic mineralogy work. To further break up mined ore, and to deter pirates, the ship is equipped with extensive laser batteries. Grapples exist to allow for ten mining seekers to dock, their crews drawing most of their supplies from the Platforms deep pockets.

Mining Platforms were transported to an active mining area by bulk carriers. Once a ship arrived, it usually spent its lifetime insystem, with surplus platforms being broken up for scrap or being sold secondhand to interested parties. The ubiquity of these vessels and their vulnerable computer systems means that potentially thousands of these ships are infected with active Viral strains. With their heavy armament they can be especially deadly to unsuspecting survey and recon crews. Caution is therefore advised when approaching or examining one of these hulls.

(Mining Platform was adapted from stats given in the old Beltstrike Module)
