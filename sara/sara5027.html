Crusader Class Troop Carrier

design by Derek Stanley

Designed soon after the outbreak of hostilities with Solee, the Crusader Class Troop Carrier is little more than a hasty conversion of the old Steller Class Subsidized Liner's of the last Imperium. Many of the modifications found in the Admiral Bertil conversion have been incorporated into the Crusader and the design itself owes much to the recovery of the original Admiral Bertil, Vigilante, from the cemetary world of Bhule.

Outwardly identical to the Stellar and Bastien Class liners of the final Imperium the Crusader has a drastically altered interior including space for a 20-ton multimission pod, three custom designed 10-ton assault lander/ground support vehicles, two drop capsule launchers capable of deploying 30 drop capsules in under five minutes and quarters for an entire company of marines. The ship is also out fitted with a fully capable sick bay including a four person emergency low berth as well as a machine shop and electronic's shop allowing the Crusader to operate independently for a period of several months under extremely unfavourable conditions.

As work on the first two Crusader refits neared completion at Trybec's Dawn Construction a pair of specially constructed 20-ton multi-mission modules were transfered from Aubaine to Trybec for fitting into the ships multi-mission bay. These new modules would incorporate a full EMM suite capable of masking the entire vessels signature, wide band EMS jammers and planetary strike missiles.

The first two Crusader's began space trials soon after Solee began landing troops on Janie and were pressed into forward duty soon after New Year's 1203. Originally intended as an RCES vessel for hot recoveries the Crusader's were used throughout the Soleean crisis to deliver Reformation Coalition special forces teams to worlds utilized by Solee as forward staging area's.
