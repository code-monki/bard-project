Stellar Subsidized Liner

design by Peter Gray

The Type M Subsidised Liner was once the staple of the spaceways. Though not as capacious as proprietary designs of the larger spacelines, its simplicity and abundant availability made it a favorite of smaller shipping companies and well heeled free traders. The concept behind a subsidised ship is fairly easy: in return for a stipend provided by either larger companies or planetary governments, the crews or owner/operators of these vessels were contractually obligated to service a particular trade route. On the mains the smaller Type R Fat Trader predominated, with the Type M taking cargo and passenger responsibilities in less dense stellar regions.

Three main classes existed: The TL-12 Bastien, the TL-13 Bertil and the TL-15 Stellar, the latter being the most popular and common. The main external difference between these classes were in the arrangement of the armament, and the stowage method for the ship's launch. On the Stellar, the launch is carried in a streamlined grapple along the ship's spine, while the others carried it in an internal hangar. The Stellars were most common in rimward sectors of the Third Imperium, such as Sol and Diaspora, and in coreward sectors like Deneb and Corridor, areas where few J-1 mains existed for the smaller Type R. The continuing popularity of this class is evident in the hundreds of unofficial conversions of older Stellars constructed since the Collapse, from pirate and smuggling vessels to the White Angel hospital and relief vessels.

(This, with the exception of a handful of small craft, completes the CT Basic Ship set.)
