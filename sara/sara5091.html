Erne Corvette

design by Peter Gray

The Erne class corvette is a Regency 1132 design. Inspired by the post Fifth War introduction of the Zhodani Friants corvette, the Admiralty issued a specification for a new small warship to supplement the stock patrol cruiser for convoy escort and system patrol duties. The specification was issued in late 1115, and bids from prospective manufacturers were still being collected when Dulinor sparked the Imperial Civil War. LSP of Starn was one of the bidders, but had to shelve the design for the next fifteen years. In the aftermath of the Collapse it finally built a prototype with its own money, and shrewdly marketed it to several systems that now made up the Trailing frontier of the Regency. After Deneb bought about sixty, the Regency Navy became reinterested in the class, and assigned the hundreds of ships it recieved to reserve or inactive duty naval personnel, and sent thousands more as technical aid to various planetary navys and contractors.

The Erne is named after a nautical bird of prey native to old Terra. Only the two barbettes are fitted during private or reserve service, leaving the turrets for customisation, usually sandcasters. Missles are fitted during regular line service, and an additional gunner (and MFD) is carried. The Erne may be substituted for the Patrol Cruiser during mustering out for Regency Naval characters. Personnel who recieve this vessel are considered to be part of the inactive reserve, and are subject to recall for life.
