KiLEEeekaa Shellcracker
Age 37 in 1201

by Rob Beck

    Occupation: Aubaini Assemblyman - leader of the Aubani delegation
      Chair of the Internal Affairs Committee
    Attributes: A68C89-B (out of water 568C89-B)
    Background:
      KiLEEeekaa was born on Aubaine on 15/III/1164. She passed the rigorous testing necessary to attend school on dry land where she focused on learning about government and the history of other interstellar governments. She later attended law school with a hope to go into politics. After graduation, she ran for and was handily elected to the Vras Assembly as one of the junior Schalli delegation. She was outspoken in her desire to expand the Vras trading fleet to establish firmer links with Vras' neighbor systems. With the arrival of the Hiver, her views found a whole new forum and she advocated full cooperation with them. Although there were those who questioned the wisdom of relying on an alien species, it was Schalli like KiLEEeekaa who provided the example and the calm voice that was needed to win over the population. KiLEEeekaa was also a major proponent of the Hiver Technical Academy and was one of the chief lobbyists for it being on the newly christened Aubaine, this more out of pride for her homeworld than anything else. She was one of the first delegates of the League of the New Dawn and when the original twelve ships did not return, it was her voice as one of the few who remained calm and helped provide direction and comfort in time of sorrow. KiLEEeekaa knew that her society could not die with those twelve crews, and made it her lifequest to ensure that their journey would not have been in vain and that they would not be forgotten. KiLEEeekaa's famous "Path of Tears" speech shortly after the founding of the Reformation Coalition best summed up the determination and spirit of the new government and the need to push ever forward. Her charisma and notoriety during this period elevated her to the position of Chairperson of Internal Affairs, a committee which oversees the dealings and smooth relations of the varied member worlds of the Coalition. Here she has put her gift for eloquence and her ability to get the job done to it's best use.

    Appearance:
      KiLEEeekaa is fairly average looking as shallow-water Schalli go. She has a rather pink muzzle with a deep, almost metallic blue body. Two long yellow streaks run down the length of her body, denoting her shallow water heritage.

      Her face is rather unique and her most striking features are her wide-set eyes and rather broad snout. She has a habit of flicking her face whiskers when she gets annoyed at something.

    Personality:
      KiLEEeekaa is a warm and outgoing sort who likes to make others feel at ease around her. She exudes a calming presence just by entering a room. In public, she is gracious, polite, and ever the statesperson. In her private chambers or personal meetings, she is warm but business-like, and possessed of a determination to address whatever is asked of her. In the privacy of the sea, she is relaxed and carefree. Only then does she shed the aura of statesmanship and allow her true persona to peek out. To those who know her best, there is an inward sadness. KiLEEeekaa is not unaware of the turmoils her people face, and keeps in close touch with her family and oldest friends, always on the look out for new information. Occupied as she is with keeping the Coalition together, she feels helpless when she confronts the fact that she cannot even help to maintain the unity of her own people.

      The only time KiLEEeekaa will ever become truly upset and fearsome is when dealing with those that would try to destroy that which she has helped to create. She does not just look upon foreign governments and Virus as the chief threats to the Coalition, but those who would damage it from within. This includes factions such as the Humanists, led by Jonas Bigalow, and the feudal technocrats who seek a more centrist and less free form of government for the Coalition member worlds.

    Motivation:
      Spade Ace: KiLEEeekaa is a highly charismatic individual and can win over some of the most stubborn souls. This manifests itself as a natural eloquence she possesses and an aura of confidence and ability. She is not shy about this ability either and uses it to champion her beliefs.
      Heart 9: KiLEEeekaa is strongly motivated to improve the condition of all those around her. This is her reason for entering public service. She is that rare instance of someone who, at no personal benefit to herself, would like to see the quality of life elevated for all other sentient beings. This also manifests itself as a strong desire to see the Reformation Coalition and all that it stands for survive and prosper. She truly believes that it is this corner of the galaxy's best hope for restoring civilization without returning to the callous disregard of the rights of the individual and the devaluation of life that existed in the Imperium. She also strongly opposes those that would corrupt or distort that dream. She is a bitter enemy of those who would use the Coalition's resources for personal gain or to inflict pain on the innocent.
    Refereeing KiLEEeekaa:
      KiLEEeekaa is the epitome of the phrase "tough old broad". While to her friends she is suitably warm and fuzzy and to the people she is the champion of their ideals, to her enemies she is a ruthless and dangerous opponent. It takes a strong character to do the job she does. The Coalition is a diverse place with many different cultures and ideals, and it takes a lot of effort to keep these variables working together. This is the job KiLEEeekaa excels at. Her gift for rhetoric is only matched by her gift for compromise. She, however, will never cut a deal that she feels is harmful to individual sentient's rights or to the Coaliton as a whole. It is also KiLEEeekaa's job to integrate conquered or recently admitted worlds into the Coalition infrastructure. While the Foreign Relations board seeks to secure the best and most ruthless deals with the Coaliton's neighbors, so as to show them who is the master and who the servant, KiLEEeekaa's Internal Affairs board is charged with bringing new members into the fold as gently and smoothly as possible. These worlds are made to feel welcome and are almost coddled in the attempt to make the transferrance from foreigner to member world as quickly and easily as possible. Probably the two areas of conflict the KiLEEeekaa is involved with is the annoyance that is the Humanists Party and the real threat, she feels, to the future of the Reformation Coalition, the Centrists Party. The Humanists' leader, Jonas Bigalow, and KiLEEeekaa have had legendary wars of words with one another on the floor of the Assembly, some speeches going on for hours at a time. In private, they tend to avoid each other, speaking only when they must, that being at times when new worlds are being brought into the Coalition. It is at these times that the real war rages, as they are not constrained by the decorum of the Assembly hall and can let each know how they truly feel. Their relationship is one that will never reach true physical violence, but one that will not allow these two to look upon each other as anything less than the biggest thorn in their respective sides. Aside from this little diversion, KiLEEeekaa must deal with the Centrists and their attempt to remold the Coalition into their image. Because she is the senior Aubani delegate, it is she to which other Federalists look to as the chief proponent of their cause in the Assembly. Despite her other duties, she is fully prepared and capable to step forward at a moments notice to defend any piece of Federalist legislation, and debate with her every breath each piece of Centrist legislation. Her speeches in favor of the Federalist position are legendary, and her status as a founding member of the Assembly lends even more weight to her arguments. Currently, she is occupied in debates regarding the Ship Bill, which has confused the traditional lines of Centrist and Federalist. KiLEEeekaa is in favor of the bill, but understands the misgivings others feel that this might lead to an increased central authority. She sees it as better than allowing too much power to be concentrated in the hands of a few technocrats, a situation which might bring the problem of too much central authority to light on its own. Her other concern is the call for a constitutional convention. She is not sure yet how she will handle this issue yet, but she knows it will likely be the toughest challenge she has ever had to face.
