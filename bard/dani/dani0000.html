Vareese Kerby
Age 41 in 1201

by Rob Beck

    Occupation: Oriflamme Assemblyman - Leader of the Antisubinfeudationists
    Attributes: 766998-B
    Background:
      Vareese was born on Oriflamme on 06/IX/1160. He attended the Nemyer Refounded University (NRU) right out of secondary school, focusing on administrative skills. He then attended law school but didn't take the bar, hoping instead to secure a lucrative spot in a corporation. Vareese managed to land a position with Waldmann Trust, but proved to be a poor money maker. He became quickly disillusioned with the boring life that his job in the feudal structure provided him, and decided to move on to the Technarch Council, using family influence. He proved to be a charismatic leader and rose quickly in the Technarch Council. When the call came out for representatives to the new Dawn League, he saw this as a way to further his career and serve the cause of promoting his form of government in the new interstellar government. He was easily appointed and stayed on through the change to the Reformation Coalition. Since then, he has viewed what he calls a "softening" of some of the F-tech representatives in the Assembly, and believes they no longer seek what is best for the RC, which is technology controlled by those of proper position and nobility. Recently, he has come to head the small, but vocal minority of Assemblymen who feel that feudal holdings should not be divided up ever amongst employees and that the technarch-class must be preserved at all costs.

    Appearance:
      Vareese Kerby is a man of fairly average build and height. He is ever so slightly out of shape, do more to age than lack of activity. He has black hair and brown eyes, which are set somewhat close together, and his nose is thin and not very pronounced. Vareese has a mild overbite, but this doesn't show unless observed very closely. His speech tends to be a little on the quiet side. His demeanor is a little stiff and one does not feel immediately at ease around him. He walks with an air of superiority, bred into him as a member of a strong technarch family.
    Personality:
      Vareese is typical of Oriflammen upper class in that he's very self-assured and confident of his own superiority. His attitude is a bit uppity, but not so much as to be overbearing. He is friendly and polite when the situation is called for but can be cold and not very attentive if his mind is on something else. Vareese shows a certain discomfort when forced to deal with those he considers beneath him, specifically, economically or socially beneath him. Amongst his peers, he can be quite animated and cheerful, though and can hold a conversation on just about any topic. On matters that concern him, he is dedicated and thoughtful. In these situations, he gives those involved his undivided attention and will do all in his power to see that a solution is found to their problem. Outside of those things important to him, he is not very responsive, however.
    Motivation:
      Spade Jack: Vareese is a pompous, arrogant soul who is firmly convinced of his and his class' superiority to all those around him. He makes no bones about his feelings and is convinced that his beliefs are the way it should be.
      Heart 6: This is moderated by his strong sense of duty to the Assembly. He genuinely wants to see the Coalition succeed and in his own way respects all its members, even though most do not agree with his "Natural Order". He realizes that not all Assemblymen are of his stature, but he knows they're the best the member planets have to offer, and for the time being, that will have to be good enough.
    Refereeing Kerby:
      Vareese Kerby is, on the surface, quite simple. He is a natural aristocrat, seeking to preserve the priviledge and power that come with his position. The true depth of his personality, however, lies in his dedication to making the dream of the Coalition succeed. Kerby is as dedicated as the most hot-blooded Federalists to preserving and protecting the RC; he just has a different view of how the RC should be protected. To him, the RC can only survive by concentrating the technological power it has into as few capable hands as possible. To squander such wonders on commoners and foreign governments is folly, when they can be put to such good use at home by those who, by birthright, are capable of best utilizing them. His relation to other Assemblymen, as stated before, is that he considers them equals, just that some are more equal than others. He makes it his business to get in the way of legislation that would put the Technarchs at even the slightest risk and/or give more freedoms to common citizens regarding technology or feudal holdings. His inability to reach beyond this, however, has led to his lack of total support even among his fellow Oriflammen delegates. He has gathered around him, though, the few that agree with his logic and allies himself with people like Jonas Bigalow when he finds pieces of common ground he can cling to. Jonas finds it useful to offer a simple appeasement to Vareese in the form of some minor concession or some tacit agreement with his policies in order to win a few extra votes here and there. Those that do not agree with Kerby's position find it difficult to tolerate him for extended periods, as his true personality tends to wear through after prolonged exposure.
