Unarmed Martial Arts

by Franklin W. Cain

1.0 New/Revised Skills and Cascades

Armed Martial Arts (STR) -- Melee: This cascade skill now has the additional new cascade Other.

  • Other: This cascade covers all unusual or exotic melee weapons not covered by the "usual" cascades of Armed M.A., from a barbarian warrior's shield to an adventuring archeologist's bullwhip, and anything in between. This cascade *cannot* be the primary cascade of this skill; a character wanting such a specialization may, at the GM's discretion, declare a *specific* weapon type to be the primary cascade, but in such an instance, that specific weapon type becomes its own cascade of this skill.

    (Example): Dr. Montana Smith is an expert with the bullwhip. Assuming he has level 8 with this weapon, it would be recorded as "Armed Martial Arts (Special -- Bullwhip) 8," with the cascades Large Blade, Small Blade, Club, Polearm, and Other being level 4 (default).

Unarmed Martial Arts (STR) -- Melee: This skill is now a cascade skill. The cascades are Block, Strike, and Throw.

  • Block: This cascade covers martial blocks, dodges, and evades.

  • Strike: This cascade covers martial punches, kicks, and strikes.

  • Throw: This cascade covers martial throws and grapples.

2.0 New Combat Maneuvers

Disarm: This is a Formidable test of Armed Martial Arts; if the target character is using a two-handed weapon, the Disarm attempt becomes Impossible.

  • Outstanding Success: The target character loses his weapon unless he succeeds at a Formidable test of STR.

  • Success: The target character loses his weapon unless he succeeds at a Difficult test of STR.

  • Failure: The Disarm attempt fails.

  • Catastrophic Failure: The Disarm attempt fails.

Feint: This is a Difficult test of both Acting and (Armed/Unarmed) Martial Arts ("Armed M. A." if melee combat; "Unarmed M. A. if HTH combat).

  • Outstanding Success: The target character has a penalty of +1 Difficulty Level to avoid the character's next attack this turn (e.g., +1 Diff. Level to Avoid Leaping Kick if next attack is Leaping Kick, etc.).

  • Success: Same effect as Outstanding Success above, unless the target character succeeds at a Difficult test of both Observation and (Armed/ Unarmed) Martial Arts.

  • Failure: No effect.

  • Catastrophic Failure: The attacking character has given *himself* the penalty that the target character would have gotten from Outstanding Success above.

Shield Block: This is an Average test of the Other cascade of Armed Martial Arts (exception: a character who chose to specify "Shield" as a specific non-standard weapon may use that special cascade instead). The character must be using a medium-sized shield (about a meter wide); smaller shields ("bucklers") must instead be used as melee weapons performing the standard maneuver Block. Unlike other maneuvers in melee combat, Shield Block is a *free* maneuver (i.e., it doesn't count against the character's Initiative-based limits on actions).

  • Outstanding Success: Anyone trying to attack this character in melee combat has a penalty of +1 Difficulty Level.

  • Success: Same effect as Outstanding Success above.

  • Failure: No effect.

  • Catastrophic Failure: The defending character cannot make an attack this turn.

    "Asset1 & Asset2": Use the *lower* of the two assets.
    "Asset1 or Asset2": Use the *higher* of the two assets.
